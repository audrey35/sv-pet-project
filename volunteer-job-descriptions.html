Department: Animal Operations

Cat Foster Program Manager

Responsibilities: Involved in intake process of cats into the foster program. This includes coordinating with the Shelter Assessment Team to post pleas and match cats with appropriate fosters, and facilitating their move into foster care. Assigns both a foster parent and foster coordinator to each cat depending on needs of cats and internal foster grading system. Acts as escalation person for foster coordinators when in need of direction.

Requirements: Ability to multitask and be very responsive via phone and internet. Must be available during hours that rescue is making assessments.

Time Commitment: 8-10 hours per week

Kitten Foster Program Manager

Responsibilities: Involved in intake process of kittens into the foster program. This includes coordinating with the Shelter Assessment Team to post pleas and match kittens with appropriate fosters, and facilitating their move into foster care. Assigns both a foster parent and foster coordinator to each kitten/group of kittens depending on needs of kittens and internal foster grading system. Acts as escalation person for foster coordinators when in need of direction.

Requirements: Ability to multitask and be very responsive via phone and internet. Must be available during hours that rescue is making assessments.

Time Commitment: 8-10 hours per week

Dog Foster Program Manager

Responsibilities: Involved in intake process of dogs into the foster program and onboarding new dog foster parents into SVPP's foster program. Duties include reviewing dog foster applications; interviewing foster parents and providing them with all necessary materials to become part of our SVPP Foster program; coordinating with the Shelter Assessment Team to post pleas and match dogs with appropriate fosters, and facilitating their move into foster care. The Manager also assigns both a foster parent and foster coordinator to each dog depending on needs of dogs and internal foster grading system. Acts as escalation person for foster coordinators when in need of direction.

Requirements: Ability to multitask and be very responsive via phone and internet. Must be available during hours that rescue is making assessments. Must have previous dog fostering experience.

Time Commitment: 8-10 hours per week

Foster Parent Recruiter

Responsibilities: Bring new foster parent volunteers into the program by determining innovative recruiting methods. Process inquiries and screen applicants.

Requirements: Recruiting and/or customer service experience desired. A keen understanding of both people and pets. We are looking for individuals who share a strong passion for SVPP’s mission and a deep understanding of the requirements of a successful foster parent volunteer.

Time Commitment: 8-10 hours per week

Shelter Animal Assessor

Responsibilities: Assist with the behavioral assessment of dogs/cats at the shelter and interact with shelter staff members and volunteers.

Requirements: Should have knowledge of animal behavior and be comfortable handling dogs/cats with which they have no history (preferred training/behaviorist credentials required). Will represent SVPP and foster a strong working relationship with the shelter.

Time Commitment: Flexible schedule; up to 20 hrs/month

Multiple openings

Shelter Liaison

Responsibilities: Assist with identifying animals currently at the shelter who are at risk. Provide additional information about their situation (both medical and behavioral), and work in conjunction with Shelter Assessor Team.

Requirements: Should be an active shelter volunteer in good standing and will abide by shelter requirements.

Time Commitment: Flexible schedule; up to 10 hrs/month

Multiple openings

Foster Coordinator/Buddy

Responsibilities: First point of contact with foster parent. Will deliver animal to the foster family, provide initial supplies and equipment and reiterate instructions from orientation regarding marketing needs, etc. Must respond to foster parent’s questions via email or phone in a timely manner. You will work closely with other members of the foster team to assist the foster parent in all aspects of getting the animal ready for adoption. Build strong and supportive relationship with foster parents to ensure open communication and encourage them to consider fostering on a regular basis. May be involved in meet & greet with potential adopters. Does initial post-adoption follow up (after 3 days).

Requirements: Must have previous fostering experience. Customer service, organizational skills and have strong social and communication skills.

Time Commitment: 8-10 hours per week

Multiple openings

Foster Parent

Responsibilities: Responsible for the daily care of a foster animal, including feeding, exercising, playtime, socialization, grooming, reinforcing basic obedience commands and, of course, providing patience, love and security. SVPP will provide all necessary medical care and our Foster Teams will provide full support throughout the foster period. Any additional expenses incurred by the foster parent may be tax deductible. Must be willing to work with the pet to help them get ready for adoption. This may include housebreaking, basic training, socialization, etc. You may also need to transport the foster animal to scheduled medical appointments and/or adoption events and photo shoots as well as administer any required medications. Providing foster care will certainly be a rewarding experience as you are enabling the animal to go on to the greatest adventure – a place where he/she will be cherished and loved until a great forever home is found.

Requirements: To be a successful foster parent, you will need a compassionate nature, flexibility, willingness to follow instructions and interest and/or knowledge of animal behavior. Frequent email updates are required, and responsiveness to our team is critical to the success of our foster program. Reliable computer, internet service and transportation. A fenced yard is preferred, but not necessary for dog foster parents. Cat/kitten fosters are indoor only.

Time Commitment:

Foster Appreciation Coordinator

Responsibilities: Provide support and assistance in our SVPP foster program to ensure that our greatest asset, our foster parents, and the pets they foster, are getting the support they need. Examples include providing unsolicited care packages to our pets in foster care (like Bark Boxes for both dogs and cats), and assisting our SVPP Foster Team with a variety of tasks.

Requirements: We are looking for creative, up beat, animal loving humans who like working within a team.

Time Commitment: 2 - 4 hours a week, varies

Home Visit Volunteer

Responsibilities: Visit potential adopters and/or fosters in their home environment to assess safety and suitability for prospective adoptable or foster pets. For foster parents, volunteer will review program requirements and have foster parent sign foster parent release form. Visits should be no more than 30 minutes and checklist will be provided.

Requirements: Reliable transportation, drivers license and car insurance. Good interpersonal and customer service skills.

Time Commitment: As needed, varies

Multiple openings

Dog Training Specialist

Responsibilities: Work closely with the Foster Coordinator to assist with any dog behavior issues that arise. Maintain standard operating procedures for common behavior issues that occur with the rescue animals. Keep in close contact with the assigned foster family to be sure recommended training is taking place and adjust program as needed until the animal is adopted.

Requirements: Must have prior behavior and training experience handling dogs.

Time Commitment: 15 hrs/month

Multiple openings

Dog Handler

Responsibilities: Dog handlers are needed at our adoption events to manage leashed dogs, introduce dogs to potential adoptive families, walk dogs, and give lots of love and affection. This is the best opportunity for the dogs to be seen by the public and possibly find their forever home. You will be there to be the advocate for the dog. May also provide support in socialization of foster dogs.

Requirements: Dog lover with prior experience handling dog with knowledge and ability to read dog body language. Must have a valid driver’s license to transport dogs when necessary, Will require orientation and training.

Time Commitment: 2-6 hours per month, mostly on weekends

Multiple openings

Dog Training Resource Administrator

Responsibilities: Oversee administration of dog training offerings, including training classes and training/behavior partner referral list. Work with Foster Program Manager to recommend appropriate trainers/behaviorists when the need arises. Assist in conducting dog foster parent orientation classes. Suggest continuing dog behavior education opportunities for volunteers.

Adopter Follow-Up Program

* This is a Virtual Opportunity

Responsibilities: Act as a liaison between SVPP and adopters by providing continued post-adoption support. Contact adopters periodically to check on how the pet is doing in its new home.

  • 3 weeks post adoption
  • 3 months post adoption
  • 1 yr post adoption by mail
Provide any necessary support or resources and answer any questions.

Requirements: Strong organizational and follow through skills. Volunteers should fully understand the adoption process and enjoy working with people.

Time Commitment: Ongoing commitment; flexible

Animal Transport

Responsibilities: Transport volunteers use their personal vehicles to transport animals and/or supplies (i.e. rescue pick up, chauffeuring animals to/from medical or therapy visits, picking-up donations or helping with special event transport). Errands/pick up donations/drop off supplies. Travel is primarily in the San Jose area. Mileage reimbursement provided per SVPP policy.

Requirements: Must have a reliable vehicle that is licensed and insured; the vehicle must have enough space for kenneled animals. Requires a valid driver’s license.

Time Commitment: Flexibility of schedule; 2-4 hrs/week.

Multiple openings

Transport Coordination Manager

Responsibilities: Coordinate logistics of transport needs on behalf of the organization (i.e. rescue pick up, chauffeuring animals to/from medical or therapy visits, picking-up donations or helping with special event transport). Be the main point of contact to reach out to volunteers with transport needs.

Requirements: Ability to multitask, very organized, good at logistics, positive can do attitude!

Time Commitment: Flexibility of schedule; 2-4 hrs/week (varies)

Vet Services Manager

Responsibilities:Assist in establishing and maintaining vet partnerships to facilitate reduced costs for animal care. Partner with Finance to ensure that proper analysis of vet expenditures is completed. Work closely with the Vet Services Coordinator to ensure medical providers have the ability to respond to SVPP needs.

Requirements: Strong negotiation and interpersonal skills. Working knowledge of potential services and costs.

Time Commitment: 5-15 hrs/month

Vet Services Coordinator

Responsibilities: Provide support to Vet Services Manager. Follow up with foster parents regarding necessary vaccinations, vet appointments, medical records, etc.

Requirements: Volunteer must have access to email and phone and be able to respond during working hours. Requires knowledge of common canine and feline health issues.

Time Commitment: 5-15 hrs/month

Multiple openings

Adoption Coordinator (Cat)

* This is a Virtual Opportunity

Responsibilities: Provide excellent customer service and assist adopters/potential adopters with their needs. Respond to general inquiries about assistance with pets and respond to general phone/email inquiries coming into the organization; answer questions about SVPP cats/kittens, policies and general information. Assist in finalizing adoptions by uploading adoption agreements into database. Provide adopters with electronic copies of all medical records. Register microchips and provide this information with additional post adoption support resources. Update data fields in database.

Requirements: Superior customer service and responsiveness. Needs to have access to email throughout the day. Volunteers should have a solid working knowledge of SVPP; its history, mission and procedures as well as solid knowledge of the current inventory.

Time Commitment: Ongoing commitment; flexible

Adoption Events Coordinator

Responsibilities: Coordinate with partners and foster parents for adoption events at venues in San Jose and surrounding cities. Responsibilities also include posting adoption events on, SVPP website, Facebook and notification to Community Outreach and Social Media Coordinators of events to publicize. Updates internal database (Rescuegroups.org) regarding attendance of pets at each event.

Requirements: Coordinator should have a solid working knowledge of Silicon Valley Pet Project; it’s history, mission and procedures. Strong organizational and customer service skills.

Time Commitment: Time Commitment vary depending on calendar of Adoption Events.
