Department:  Animal Operations

Cat Foster Program Manager

Responsibilities: Involved in intake process of cats into the foster program. This includes coordinating with the Shelter Assessment Team to post pleas and match cats with appropriate fosters, and facilitating their move into foster care. Assigns both a foster parent and foster coordinator to each cat depending on needs of cats and internal foster grading system. Acts as escalation person for foster coordinators when in need of direction.  

Requirements: Ability to multitask and be very responsive via phone and internet. Must be available during hours that rescue is making assessments.

Time Commitment : 8-10 hours per week

Foster Coordinator/Buddy

Responsibilities:    First point of contact with foster parent. Will deliver animal to the foster family, provide initial supplies and equipment and reiterate instructions from orientation regarding marketing needs, etc.  Must respond to foster parent’s questions via email or phone in a timely manner. You will work closely with other members of the foster team to assist the foster parent in all aspects of getting the animal ready for adoption.  Build strong and supportive relationship with foster parents to ensure open communication and encourage them to consider fostering on a regular basis.  May be involved in meet & greet with potential adopters.  Does initial post-adoption follow up (after 3 days).

Requirements   : Must have previous fostering experience. Customer service, organizational skills and have strong social and communication skills.

 

Time Commitment       : 8-10 hours per week

Foster Parent

 

Responsibilities:    Responsible for the daily care of a foster animal, including feeding, exercising, playtime, socialization, grooming, reinforcing basic obedience commands and, of course, providing patience, love and security. SVPP will provide all necessary medical care and our Foster Teams will provide full support throughout the foster period. Any additional expenses incurred by the foster parent may be tax deductible. Must be willing to work with the pet to help them get ready for adoption. This may include housebreaking, basic training, socialization, etc. You may also need to transport the foster animal to scheduled medical appointments and/or adoption events and photo shoots as well as administer any required medications. Providing foster care will certainly be a rewarding experience as you are enabling the animal to go on to the greatest adventure – a place where he/she will be cherished and loved until a great  forever home is found.

 

Requirements:    To be a successful foster parent, you will need a compassionate nature, flexibility, willingness to follow instructions and interest and/or knowledge of animal behavior.  Frequent email updates are required, and responsiveness to our team is critical to the success of our foster program. Reliable computer, internet service and transportation. A fenced yard is preferred, but not necessary for dog foster parents. Cat/kitten fosters are indoor only.

Multiple openings

The   Dancing Cat   (T DC) is a project of Silicon Valley Pet Project and dedicated to improving   the lives of homeless cats by finding new and innovative ways to connect them with loving forever homes. TDC will provide a place for cat lovers to socialize and connect with like-minded people to create community and share their passion.

The Dancing Cat   is a permanent venue where cats will roam in a comfortable, home-like environment, and where people can interact with adoptable cats and each other.  The space will offer an attractive and relaxed environment as an alternative to an animal shelter for individuals and families who are seeking adoption.  T   he Dancing Cat   venue     will also provide a creative space for educational, entertaining, and community-building events open to the public.  

 

TDC strives to offer volunteers a rewarding, educational and fun experience.  As an integral member of the team, there will be opportunities to learn new skills and/or apply your expertise to our cause, meet wonderful people in our community and have some fun while you are saving lives.

Cat Care

Cat Foster Parent

Responsibilities:    Responsible for the daily care of a foster animal, including feeding, exercising, playtime, socialization, grooming, reinforcing basic obedience commands and, of course, providing patience, love and security.  You will be provided all necessary medical care and full support throughout the foster period. Any additional expenses incurred by the foster parent may be tax deductible.

Requirements:    To be a successful foster parent, you will need a compassionate nature, flexibility and some knowledge of animal behavior.  Frequent email updates are required, and responsiveness to our team is critical to the success of our foster program. Reliable computer, internet service and transportation.

Time Commitment   : This position is for TDC cats that might need time to heal from sickness, or be given a break.  We expect that the time commitment will be less than a month.
