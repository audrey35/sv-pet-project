Vivian's Story

After graduating from the University of Dallas with a Masters in Politics, Vivian worked as a Development Research Specialist at the University of Dallas. She also served as the Associate Director of Major Gifts and Development Research with the Boy Scouts of America.

Vivian has always cared deeply about animal and human rights. As a seasoned advocate and volunteer with great determination, passion and energy, she has devoted her time to many volunteer efforts including Men’s Homeless Shelter, American Women's Group, Federation of American Women's Club and St. Francis Animal Protection Society.

Serving on the SVPP Board of Directors has allowed her to follow her life-long passion by taking on a more active role in advocacy for animals.

My animal rescue story begins with my husband. In 1989 he found a tiny, emaciated kitten alone in a field and took her home. We were living in Dallas, TX at the time and little did I know but that kitten (who lived with us for the next 23 years) was the beginning of what I consider my life’s work. We were living near a college campus and for the first time, I encountered feral and community cat colonies. I was shocked by the number of cats (many of whom were clearly former pets) that were struggling to survive – and at the same time were multiplying in large numbers every kitten season. I did not know there was a term for my activities but I began a Trap/Neuter/Return (TNR) program with the help of a very generous and compassionate veterinarian. During those six years we spayed/neutered and provided basic medical treatment to a large number of cats. I found homes for many kittens and even some of the so called “feral” adults. And yes, many of them did find their forever homes within my own family.

In 1998 we also rescued our beloved dog Blue. We found Blue running frantically across a busy, dark road one night and when we finally caught her we realized she was in terrible condition – a telephone cord tied tightly around her neck and covered in hundreds of engorged ticks. We wanted to help her but we had no intention of keeping her. Within a few days, however, she had a name and a home. This most amazing dog was my constant companion and filled our lives with joy for the next 16 years.

There have been many moves and many rescue stories since Dallas, however, during those years I was primarily acting as an independent rescuer. It wasn’t until I moved to California in 2011 that I became involved in a community of people and a rescue group (St. Francis Animal Protection Society) that allowed me to have a larger impact on saving homeless animals.
